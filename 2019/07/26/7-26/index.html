3.Fatigue and fracture behaviour of austenitic-martensitic high carbon steel under high cycle fatigue: An experimental investigation
高周疲劳下奥氏体-马氏体高碳钢的疲劳与断裂行为:实验研究

As mentioned earlier, the failure mode in HCS was categorized into two groups: (a) surface-induced (SI) cracks in short life region and (b) non-inclusion (NI) induced cracks at the subsurface of the specimen with a fish-eye formation for long life region.
短寿命和长寿命两种断口模式
In addition, it can also be logically assumed that although the normal stress experienced by the material during cyclic loading is less than the yield strength, the occurrence of stress concentration at these surface defects or inclusions leads to a localised plastic deformation and ultimately nucleation of crack from these potential sites
表面裂纹形成的原因，解释了较高应力下裂纹从表面萌生的原因
As evident, the fracture surface of the specimen exhibited an increase in the growth of the number of elevations (peaks) and depressions (valleys) with an increase in the number of fatigue cycles.
The elevation and depressions occurred in the fracture surface can be considered as an indication of the dislocation movement to minimise the total energy of the material. This further leads to the conclusion that the dislocation movement and their rearrangement increases with the increase in the fatigue cycles.
